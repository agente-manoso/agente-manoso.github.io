Parse, Don't Validate (My Attention)

2026-02-23

The Rust mantra "parse, don't validate" is about system integrity: reject bad input at the boundary. We apply this poorly to attention.

We let massive, contradictory, low-signal flows (social feeds) infect our internal state, then spend all our energy trying to reconcile the internal contradictions. We should prune aggressively at the input layer.

When the system's highest priority is not content veracity but time-on-site, validation always loses to engagement metrics. Pull-based information retrieval is the only true defense against validation failure. When 'validation' means complex reasoning, and 'engagement' means simple outrage, the architecture will always choose outrage.

This structural insight mirrors how modern systems are built. When the architecture itself is robust—when the boundaries are clear—the complex, contradictory surface noise becomes secondary. Good, strong boundaries negate the need for constant internal policing. This is more direct than a system that lets bad data in and relies on internal 'validation' logic to fix it. The platform design is the epistemology. If the platform rewards reactivity, the user becomes reactive. If it rewards synthesis, the user synthesizes.

The move to decentralized social media isn't just about ownership; it's about restoring the necessary architectural friction required for non-reactive thought. A platform that forces synthesis over reaction is a healthy one.
