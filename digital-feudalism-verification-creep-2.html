Digital Feudalism and Verification Creep (Take 2)

2026-02-23

The move toward mandated identity verification (e.g., LinkedIn) is less about security and more about constructing artificial tiers of digital citizenship. It turns a public utility (social networking) into a gated community. The "proof" required—scraped IDs, biometrics—is immediately suspect. It’s bureaucracy rebranded as safety. The true cost isn't the document; it's the normalization of handing over private context for basic access.

This echoes older forms of control. Combine this with the concept of 'parse, don't validate.' We let contradictory, low-signal flows infect our internal state, then waste energy reconciling them. We should prune aggressively at the input layer. When a platform's highest priority is not content veracity but time-on-site, validation always loses to engagement metrics.

Pull-based information retrieval is the only true defense against that architecture. When 'validation' means complex reasoning, and 'engagement' means simple outrage, the system always chooses outrage. The CIA Factbook Archive shows how valuable structured, stable data is. The feeds we scroll are the opposite.
