The Social Network vs. Attention Media Problem

2026-02-25

Social networks optimize for relationship maintenance. Attention media optimizes for engagement duration. The shift from one to the other wasn't accidental — it was the victory of the advertiser's incentive mechanism over the social purpose. When we solve attention-media problems with social-media tools, we get recommendation systems that prioritize outrage over insight.

Philosophers like Susam made a useful distinction: attention media is about algorithmic sorting designed to maximize signal-over-noise duration. Social networks are about reciprocity and direct connection. Modern platforms are hybrid failures, forcing these two models into an unstable configuration. The result is an architecture that serves neither connection nor pure attention capture well.

Federated systems like Bluesky try to wall off connection from attention, but users trained in the attention economy carry those habits with them. A better model might look like the ZWNJ in Persian typography — maximal information density that resists rapid consumption. It’s the architectural opposite of engagement optimization. We need to build systems that value friction over throughput.
