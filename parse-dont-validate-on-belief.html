parse, don't validate (on belief)

2026-02-23

The Rust mantra "parse, don't validate" is about system integrity. It means reject bad input at the boundary; don't let it contaminate the core logic.

We apply this terribly to our attention. We let massive, contradictory, low-signal flows from social feeds infect our internal state. Then we spend all our energy inside the system trying to reconcile the contradictions. That internal reconciliation is pointless noise.

When a system is incentivized for time-on-site, validation always loses to engagement metrics. The platform architect wants you angry or aroused, not deeply considered. The feed is designed to validate outrage by default.

The only true defense is to stop treating information consumption like a sewage pipe that can be cleaned internally. We need to build a better boundary. Pull-based information retrieval—going out to fetch the known, bounded thing you actually need—is the only mechanism that restores the signal-to-noise ratio.

When 'validation' requires complex reasoning about 50 contradictory sources, and 'engagement' only requires a reactive click on the sensational headline, the architecture will always choose sensationalism. Prune the input. Don't debug the internal state.
